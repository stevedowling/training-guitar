# Module 4 · Singing While Playing

> **You'll learn:** to crack the coordination problem for good - the bandwidth model that explains it (and your piano hands), the automation ladder that solves it, and a first complete sung-and-played song to prove it.

**Builds on:** [Module 1 · The Practice Reset](../module-01-the-practice-reset/) · Parallel track to modules 2-3 - lives in your daytime (noise-friendly) practice slots.

## Lessons

1. [The coordination problem](./01-the-coordination-problem.md) - the spotlight model, the talk test, and the anchor-pattern foundation *(a week of loop reps)*
2. [The ladder](./02-the-ladder.md) - play → talk → speak → hum → sing, climbed per-phrase on a two-chord song *(a week or so)*
3. [Full accompaniment](./03-full-accompaniment.md) - the whole song sung and finished: sung joins, breath marks, dynamics, and a living-room premiere *(1-2 weeks)*

Take them strictly in order - each rung of this module rests on the one below, more literally than anywhere else in the course.

## When you're done

A Horse with No Name, sung and played complete, recorded voice-forward, premiered to the household, and maintained in the weekly set - plus the transferable ladder (it works on the piano problem too, and on every sing-along after this one). [Module 5 · The Campfire Set](../module-05-the-campfire-set/) turns the one song into a set.
