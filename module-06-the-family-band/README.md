# Module 6 · The Family Band

> **You'll learn:** to make a guitar-and-drums duo actually work - arrangements that cover the missing bass, rehearsals a nine-year-old requests, and a three-song set tightened to gig readiness.

**Builds on:** [Module 3 · Rhythm Lock-in](../module-03-rhythm-lock-in/)

## Lessons

1. [Duo arranging](./01-duo-arranging.md) - living low, the never-stopping belt, hooks in the vocal gaps, and version-1.0 drum parts *(desk work + solo reps)*
2. [The rehearsal game](./02-the-rehearsal-game.md) - the band campaign: identity, recordings, games, and the motivation science that beats nagging *(a fortnight of 10-minute rehearsals)*
3. [The duo setlist](./03-the-duo-setlist.md) - three songs, edge work for two players, dress runs, and a booked date *(2-3 weeks)*

Take them in order - arrangements feed rehearsals feed the set, and the gig gets booked before the set feels ready (on purpose).

## When you're done

The band has a name, an album folder of recordings, a setlist card in her handwriting, synchronized endings, and a date on the calendar with grandparents invited by the drummer herself. Her practice problem has quietly become a scheduling problem. The gig itself happens in [Module 7](../module-07-capstone-two-setlists/).
