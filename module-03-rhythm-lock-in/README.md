# Module 3 · Rhythm Lock-in

> **You'll learn:** to keep time like a band member - honest self-measurement against the grid, the edge skills (count-ins, stops, endings) that make players sound like a band, and the craft of following a live beginner drummer.

**Builds on:** [Module 2 · First Full Songs](../module-02-first-full-songs/)

## Lessons

1. [The grid](./01-the-grid.md) - drum-loop games, the recording test, and the subdivision belt *(a week of 10-minute add-ons)*
2. [Starts, stops, and count-ins](./02-starts-stops-and-count-ins.md) - the edges: counting in at tempo, stops that stay together, endings chosen in advance *(a week)*
3. [First duo sessions](./03-first-duo-sessions.md) - the drummer-is-the-boss rule, ten-minute session shapes, and the games *(2-3 real sessions)*

Take them in order - the machines (lessons 1-2) absorb the mistakes so the human sessions (lesson 3) get the polished version of you.

## When you're done

Your placement against the grid is measured and tighter, your three finished songs start from real count-ins and land charted endings - and the band exists: named (probably badly), recorded (attempt two, session three), and with its next song already requested by the drummer. [Module 6 · The Family Band](../module-06-the-family-band/) builds on this directly; the campfire track ([Module 4](../module-04-singing-while-playing/)) runs happily in parallel.
